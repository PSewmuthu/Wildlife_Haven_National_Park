    • Wildlife

    Wildlife Haven National Park is home to a diverse range of wildlife species. Explore our wildlife gallery below:

    The red fox, with its iconic reddish-orange fur and bushy tail, is a common sight in Wildlife Haven National Park. These agile mammals are highly adaptable and can be found in various habitats, including forests, grasslands, and urban areas. Red foxes are opportunistic feeders, preying on small mammals, birds, insects, and even scavenging for food. Visitors may spot them foraging for food or catching glimpses of their swift movements across the landscape.

    The American black bear is the largest mammal found in Wildlife Haven National Park and plays a vital role in the park's ecosystem. These majestic creatures are primarily herbivores, feeding on berries, nuts, roots, and vegetation. However, they are also opportunistic feeders and may consume insects, small mammals, and carrion. While encounters with black bears are rare, visitors are reminded to maintain a safe distance and adhere to bear safety guidelines to ensure the well-being of both humans and bears.

    The bald eagle, with its striking white head and tail contrasting against dark brown plumage, is a symbol of strength and freedom. Wildlife Haven National Park provides a habitat for these magnificent birds of prey, where they soar high above the landscape, scanning for fish and other prey. Bald eagles are often seen near bodies of water, such as rivers and lakes, where they hunt for fish and build their nests in tall trees. Witnessing the sight of a bald eagle in flight is a memorable experience for visitors to the park.

    The white-tailed deer is a graceful and iconic species of Wildlife Haven National Park, known for its distinctive white tail that flashes when startled. These herbivores are commonly found in forested areas, where they feed on leaves, grasses, fruits, and nuts. White-tailed deer are most active during dawn and dusk, often seen grazing in meadows or browsing along forest edges. Visitors may encounter these elegant creatures while exploring the park's trails or enjoying scenic overlooks.

    The eastern gray squirrel is a familiar sight in Wildlife Haven National Park, with its bushy tail and gray fur. These arboreal rodents are highly adaptable and can be found in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, urban parks, and suburban neighborhoods. Eastern gray squirrels are omnivorous, feeding on a diet that includes nuts, seeds, fruits, insects, and bird eggs. Visitors may observe them scampering among the trees, caching food, or displaying their acrobatic skills as they navigate their environment.
